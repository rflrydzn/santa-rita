History

          Santa Rita, a pear-shaped town in Pampanga, with a land area of 3,296 hectares, has a rich history. It began in 1697 as a settlement called Gasac and was initially managed by Porac. In 1724, it was separated from Porac, but parochial independence was achieved in 1771. The town's church, built in 1839, was a significant accomplishment using forced labor.


          The proximity to Bacolor and the Spanish capital transfer led to names like Santa Rita de Lele and Santa Rita Baculud. Attempts to merge Santa Rita with Bacolor were made during the American occupation but were thwarted.


          Harsh times post-liberation included "Makapili" hunts and "polo" labor for plaza reconstruction. In 1951, rebels burned the municipal building. Peace came with Mayor German Galang, who served for over two decades.


          In 1991, the eruption of Mt. Pinatubo and ensuing lahar threatened Santa Rita. The construction of the Megadike and faith in patroness Apung Dita saved the town. Misa Sa Control became an annual celebration of thanksgiving.


          Today, Santa Rita stands resilient, known for its agriculture and fine delicacies. The Duman Festival, created by ArtiSta.Rita, showcases Kapampangan culture. The Cadena de Suman, initiated by Mayor Yolanda Pineda, promotes suman and unity.


          In 2011, the municipal building was reconstructed, symbolizing economic growth and reverence for tradition. Santa Rita is a popular filming location, and it possesses a first-class relic of Santa Rita de Cascia.


          This peaceful town, known for its religiosity and hospitality, is a place of endless opportunities. Santa Rita, kaluguran ning Diyos at Apung Dita; balen da reng mangasipag at matenakan; ing pagmaragul ning Pampanga!
